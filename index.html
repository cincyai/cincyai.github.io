We proudly present School of AI first Global Health Hackathon.

According to the UN Sustainable Goals ensuring healthy lives and promoting the well-being at all ages is essential to sustainable development. On March 30, 2019, join School of AI for a 24-hour global health hackathon where we will use AI models to transform the way doctors deliver cost-effective, high-quality diagnostic and treatment services to their patients.

Objective

People from different disciplines will work together in teams for one weekend and collaborate on solving pressing healthcare issues in a tangible manner. We are not only providing you with relevant challenges from the field but also with relevant data sets that can serve as a backbone for your idea on the day of the event.

What should you bring to the Hackathon?

Eventbrite registration

Either print off or have your registration ready on your phone. We'll use the barcodes to check you in.

Laptop

You'll need your laptop to work with your team to create your app for the hackathon. Datasets will be provided in cloud storage but you should still have plenty of room to download and analyze the data while you're working with it.

Chargers

Make sure you bring your chargers for your laptop and your phone. You don't want to lose power in the middle of the night!

GitHub account

At least one (1) team member should have a GitHub account. These are free and will be the place that the code for your app and presentation materials will be submitted at the end of the hackaton.

Rules

Who is eligible to win global prizes?

Teams that do not have Accenture employees

Teams that submit their code/results to GitHub (open source)

Teams that abide by the following rules

No coding prior to the Hackathon

All code needs to be written and committed to your GitHub repository within the twenty-four (24) hour period of time specified above on the schedule. After the 24 hours have passed, teams will be allowed some time to make changes to READMEs and presentation materials, but no more commits to code are allowed.

No copying of other teams' work

All custom work should be your own and completed during the hackathon. Use of open source software and published models is permitted and encouraged.

No uncited open source code (if non-team members' work)

Open source code is permitted and encouraged, but should all be cited. This may be done through conventions used by your programming language (e.g. NPM dependencies in a package.json file).

Scoring and Judging

Innovation (0 - 10)

Did the team come up with a new approach to an existing problem?

Did the team make creative use of available resources?

Did the team propose a new challenge in healthcare and create a solution?

Technical achievement (0 - 10)

Did the team complete the hack; does it work; can it be demonstrated seemlessly?

While the app doesn't have to be production ready, does the idea come to life?

Is the code scalable, maintainable, readable, etc...?

Application to healthcare problems (0 - 10)

Did the team understand the problem?

How does the app relate to healthcare?

Is this an app the people want/need?

Is this an app that the medical profession can benefit from?
